# Inspirations

What I'm pulling from each reference, and what I'm deliberately leaving
behind. Audience: engineer with a designer's eye; not a designer with an
engineer's eye.

## keyavadgama.com — type-as-system

**Pull**

- Stacked identity pattern in the hero. Keya is "Designer, typographer,
  artist, writer" then "Devoted romanticizer of letterforms" — the same
  person described three times, each refining the last.
- Colophon at the bottom: a literal "Site set in MD System ↗" with a link
  to the typeface source. Quiet, specific, signals care.
- Project case studies as visual breakdowns — color swatches with hex
  values, labeled UI artifacts ("CTA button light mode, Start Earning"),
  embedded metadata.
- Type as the lead instrument. Decoration is rare; rhythm comes from size,
  weight, and tracking.

**Leave behind**

- The actual color-swatch sections (Plum 800 / Peach 300 / Lime 400). Keya
  is a typographer talking shop with other designers. An EM showing
  hex-coded swatches reads as cosplay. Equivalent for me = a syntax
  block, a system trace, a config snippet — not Pantone chips.
- The personal-brand-as-art-object framing. I'm not selling letterforms;
  I'm selling judgment.

## hellodani.co — voice and stacked-headline rhythm

**Pull**

- Conversational section headers. Dani uses: "Step into my digital home,"
  "Tiny fraction of my work," "I've got your back with…," "Behind the
  pixels." The grammar is the move, not the exact words.
- The two-line hero break: "Visual and / Product designer you can count
  on." A noun phrase that wraps deliberately, with weight contrast
  between lines so the second line lands harder. I'll adapt this for the
  fixed hero copy.
- CTA voice: "Chat with me" instead of "Contact." Email-as-conversation.
- Industry-tag metadata on work tiles ("medtech, ai" / "b2b, SaaS"). One
  line, lowercase, comma-separated, no badges.

**Leave behind**

- "I've got your back" and the warmth-as-strategy register. Dani is a
  freelancer building trust with hiring stakeholders; I'm an EM signaling
  judgment to peers. My voice is **dry-warm**, not warm-warm. Sentences
  like "you can count on" or "I've got your back" don't fit. Closer to
  *"Here's what I've done. Here's what I'd do for you."*
- Emoji and exclamation points. Dani uses them sparingly and well — but
  the register is wrong for an EM portfolio.

## emmiwu.com — structured tiles and one quiet flourish

**Pull**

- The metadata grammar above each project tile: `[context] — [date]`,
  lowercase, en-dash, mono. Verbatim from her site: `"concept - fall
  2025"`, `"Perplexity - spring 2026"`, `"the daily Pennsylvanian -
  ongoing"`. Adapt to: `"Splunk — 2022–present"`, `"Series A startup —
  2019–2020"`.
- The closing line above the footer: `"To plant a garden, is to believe
  in the future."` — a real sentence, not a fortune-cookie. I owe Ankit
  three candidates that pass the same bar.
- The signature footer flourish: ASCII garden + `"made with <3 and lots
  of coffee"`. The pattern is *one handcrafted moment in the closing
  space*, not a sprinkle across the page.
- The `/playground` page idea — a separate room for things that don't fit
  the main arc.
- `"say hi"` as the contact section header. Two words, lowercase, no
  punctuation. Adopt the form.

**Leave behind**

- The handwritten/coffee-and-hearts register. Emmi is a student/designer
  marking her age and personality through it. For an EM in identity
  infrastructure, the equivalent flourish is **terminal-shaped**, not
  garden-shaped. Same gesture, different vocabulary.
- The student-portfolio "in progress" feel (rolling semester dates,
  ongoing projects in their first weeks). My case studies should read as
  shipped work with outcomes.

## Synthesis — three patterns this site needs

1. **Hero stacks the same idea three ways.** Keya's identity-stack + Dani's
   line-break weight contrast, applied to Ankit's fixed hero line.
2. **Editorial restraint on the page, dry warmth in the microcopy.** Type
   does the heavy lifting; section headers and CTAs do the personality.
3. **One handcrafted moment in the closing space, shaped like the work.**
   Engineer's equivalent of the ASCII garden. Picked once, built well.
